Image Approximation Using Genetic Algorithm

This project applies genetic algorithm into image approximation. The basic idea comes from Roger Johansson Blog. All the approximation images are intitially generated using 100 triagles of randomly generated colors and locations. Later, for each iteration, children will be generated from the last iteration's parent images. Part of the child image's triangles will come from the first parent, and the remaining triangles will come from the second parent. Next, mutations of chaning the vertex location of the triangles, changing the color and transparency of triangles, generating new triangles replacing old ones in the current image, and changing the relative location of triangles will be applied to the newly generated child. Finally, the firness score will be calculated, and the more similar ones will be kept, and the process repeats until a specific searching effort had been reached.
